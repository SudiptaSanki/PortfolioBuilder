THE WAY

Wabi-Sabi: Finding Grace in Decay

In the tea room, we celebrate the rustic, the asymmetric, and the natural. We use hand-carved bamboo scoops, and serve tea in cracked chawan bowls repaired with gold kintsugi. There are no mirrors, no synthetic scents, and no digital interruptions.

Every step—from measuring the mineral water temperature to the gentle whisking of Uji Matcha—is an invitation to return to the present moment.
